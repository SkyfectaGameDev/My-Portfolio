Hey! My name is Alisha, and I am 21 years old. I am an indie game developer, a content creator, and a graphics design hobbyist. People better know me through these things through my online name - "Skyfecta". This is a personal page and space to share my creations, ideas, or to simply put boards of inspiration together to assist me in creating new projects!

I've been pursuing a career in IT or game development since 2015 - which was when I figured out exactly what I wanted to do in high school. I took Computing and ICT as my options for GCSE, and fgollowed that up wiht an Interactive Media & Games course at college - where I was awarded a distinction. From there, I went to university, where I entered a Games Design & Programming course, however thanks to covid and other issues going on in my personal life, I had to pull out. Since then, I have focused on doing lots of programming and development in my own spare time as a way to build up my portfolio, and carry on with my goal to pursue a career in the IT and/or programming fields.

Alongside this, I have tought myself some graphic art skills, as being a one-person team on everything I do means I need to do everythign myself. So, over the years since around 2017, I have taught myself how to use Adobe Photoshop, GIMP, Aseprite, and even video editing software such as Wondershare Filmora and DaVinci Resolve. I mostly do this to make my hobbies stand out better, and because it saves money to know how to do this myself - but I also genuinely really enjoy it, too.

And finally, that leads on to my content creation hobby. As I stated previously, I know how to edit videos - and with that skill comes with the ability to create and share videos! I have mainly focused on video-game related media, but my editing skills go beyond that and into editing pretty much any video that needs to be done.


When I'm not behind the screen, you can often find me cooking, swimming, writing, or refurbishing old technology to bring new life into it. I'm a HUGE fan of cassettes, VHS tapes and old/obscure media formats in general. My first games console was a Sega Mega Drive, despite not growing up in that era! I had a love for 2D platformers as a kid, and when my parents noticed that they bought me one on Christmas 2005. Ever since then, my obsession with older media and games began - and the older I got, the more I knew I wanted to explore it more.
